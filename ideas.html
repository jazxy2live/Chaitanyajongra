The Archive

A history of ideas I have worked upon. They are pivoted projects, but they add up to who I am. This is a collection of what I have built, explored, and learned from.

TeensForLife

Pivoted
July 2024 — November 2024

A community platform for teenagers to find resources on mental health, personal growth, and peer support. We aimed to be a one-stop shop for solutions and tools.

(We were the Walmart for teenagers, except instead of products, we provided solutions. I pivoted because I didn't know how to navigate the execution—it was my first idea.)

Community Support

PalConnect

Pivoted
December 2024 — March 2025

A "no-social" app designed for Gen-Z to find meaningful friendships based on shared interests. The goal was to cut through the fluff of traditional social media and bridge the gap between acquaintances and true friends.

(I am really proud of this, I put my soul into it. We were close to 100 users, but I pivoted because I realized I didn't want to keep working on it.)

Social Network Gen-Z

FoundrMatch

Pivoted
March 2025 — April 2025

A platform connecting lonely founders with ambitious potential co-founders. It focused on matching individuals based on vision, skills, and values to facilitate the birth of new startups.

(I tested this initially and it really worked—people wanted it. But I stopped because I wanted to spend my time on something crazier and world-changing.)

Aurame Labs, Inc.

Pivoted
April 2025 — August 2025

An ambitious venture into AR social discovery that allowed users to connect with people and digital layers in the physical world. It aggregated various proximity-based interaction concepts.

(Adding a social layer to Augmented Reality! I registered the company as Aurame Labs, Inc., but put the idea on the shelf because it was too early and my investor ran away.)
